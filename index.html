Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Innovation is the process of creating and implementing new ideas, methods, or technologies to solve problems, improve efficiency, or add value. It drives progress by challenging conventional thinking and fostering creativity, leading to advancements in products, services, and systems.

Sustainability

Sustainability focuses on meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet theirs. It emphasizes environmental preservation, responsible resource use, and social equity for long-term harmony.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure refers to the physical and organizational structures essential for the functioning of a society, such as roads, bridges, water systems, power grids, and digital networks. It supports economic growth, enhances connectivity, and improves living standards.

Empowerment

Empowerment involves enabling individuals or groups to take control of their lives, make decisions, and access resources. It fosters self-confidence, independence, and opportunities for growth, often promoting social and economic equity.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur and economist, is renowned as the founder of Grameen Bank and the pioneer of microfinance and microcredit. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for empowering millions of impoverished people, particularly women, through access to small loans without collateral. His innovative approach revolutionized poverty alleviation and inspired social business models worldwide.
